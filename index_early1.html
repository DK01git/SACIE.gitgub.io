About Us

#Brief topic

Inclusive education promotes equal opportunities for all learners, regardless of their diverse backgrounds, abilitRRies, etc. It is to ensure that every individual has the right to access quality education within their local community, and that education should be adapted to meet the diverse needs of learners rather than expecting learners to fit into preconceived molds.

It has become a necessity to promote inclusive practices as the teachers or educators need to get updated knowledge and training to maximise inclusivity when teaching. South Asian Centre for Inclusive Education aims to address these challenges by serving as a catalyst for change in educational institutions, policy-making bodies, and communities.

By bringing together experts, educators, researchers, and stakeholders, the centre will foster collaboration and knowledge-sharing, resulting in tangible improvements in inclusive practices and policies.

Read More
Rationale

The establishment of the Centre on Inclusive Education is driven by a deep-seated commitment to addressing the educational disparities and barriers faced by diverse learners.

Mission

The Centre on Inclusive Education is dedicated to advancing inclusive educational practices through collaboration, research, and capacity building. Our mission is to empower educators, schools, and policymakers with the knowledge, skills, and resources to create inclusive learning environments that foster diversity, equity, and student success.

Vision

To create an inclusive and equitable education setup, where every learner is valued, supported, and empowered to reach their full potential.

People

Resource Persons and Commitee

Prof. Bimali Indrarathne
Bimali Indrarathne is an Associate Professor in Applied Linguistics in the Department of Education, University of York, United Kingdom. Prior to that, she worked at King’s College London and Lancaster University, UK. Her main research interest is Second Language Acquisition, and she has worked on several projects investigating second language input processing, vocabulary acquisition and English for academic purposes.
Dr. Aminath Shafiya Adam
Dr. Shafiya is a Maldivian, completed her Doctoral Degree in Education in 2015, with an international scholarship awarded by The University of Waikato, New Zealand. Currently, she works as an assistant professor at the Faculty of Education. Maldives National University (MNU). Her role involves teaching postgraduate papers, supervising postgraduate students’ research projects, and managing research projects. Dr. Shafiya is an experienced researcher. Some of her research work involves ICT integration in teaching, leadership research, and the use of social media. Dr Shafiya is actively involved in writing for publications, reviewing papers for academic journals, and presenting research work in conferences both at national and international level. She has written widely about inclusive practice and special needs in local platforms. At present, she is working on two books in the local language, one related to inclusive practice and another on assistive technology.
Prof. Laxman Gnawali
Prof. Laxman Gnawali, PhD, previously taught the English language at primary, secondary and tertiary levels in rural and urban parts of Nepal for fifteen years. After his graduate studies in Teacher Training for ELT at the University of Exeter, UK as Hornby Scholar, he came to the field of teacher education and training. He now leads English language teacher education programmes at Kathmandu University. He currently serves NELTA as its Senior Vice President as well as Deputy Editor-in-Chief of the Journal of NELTA.
Prof Dr Nasreen Hussain
Prof Dr Nasreen Hussain, Director of Research & Training at iRAPA brings teaching, administrative, research, and consultancy experience to the academia. She has edited books as well as written book chapters and articles, apart from receiving national and international awards for recognition of her distinguished academic career in diversified areas. Presently, she is the Editor-in-Chief of Academy of Education and Social Sciences Review, which is an online journal. Her forte is in qualitative research methods, teacher education, TESOL/TEFL, curriculum design and development, strategic educational planning, and organizational learning at tertiary level.
Dr. Yangdon
Dr. Yangdon is a teacher educator at Samtse College of Education, Royal university of Bhutan. She worked as a teacher prior to becoming a teacher educator. She teaches both English and professional modules. She did her Masters in Curriculum Studies from the University of New Brunswick, Canada and Doctor of Philosophy from the University of New England, Australia. She has facilitated workshops in various areas including English Curriculum, Non-Formal Education (NFE), and Raising Teachers' Awareness of Learning Difficulties and Inclusive Practices. Dr Yangdon actively participates in educational, social, and action research activities, furthering her contributions to the field. She has published papers in international journals. She received the Endeavour Scholarship Award to pursue PhD at the University of New England, Australia. Passionate about teaching, Dr. Yangdon firmly believes in being a supportive guide rather than a dominant figure in the classroom. Her dedication to empowering students and fostering their growth is evident in her teaching philosophy.
Dr. Akhter Jahan
Dr. Akhter Jahan is an Associate Professor at the Department of English, East West University, Bangladesh. She is currently the chairperson of the department. She has a Ph.D. in English Language Studies from International Islamic University Malaysia (IIUM). She studied MA in Language Teaching: TESOL at the University of Lancaster, UK winning the Hornby Scholarship (2011-2012). She works as a Teacher Trainer for the British Council Dhaka. She has also reviewed books published by the National Curriculum and Textbook Board (NCTB) of Bangladesh. She is a current executive committee member and the former treasurer of the Bangladesh English Language Teachers Association (BELTA). She has published and presented research at different conferences at both home and abroad. She received training on raising teachers’ awareness about dyslexia and inclusive practices in January 2023 in Maldives under a project funded by the University of York, UK. After completing that program, she trained 40 primary-level school teachers in Bangladesh on that same topic. She is also the coordinator of the young learners’ SIG of BELTA. Her interest lies in Second language Acquisition, ELT, Teacher Education, and Materials Development.
Major Wimansha Abeyawickrama
Major Wimansha Abeyawickrama is a Senior Lecturer in the Department of Languages, General Sir John Kotelawala Defence University, Sri Lanka. He is currently the Head of the Department. He is an experienced ELT teacher in several contexts and also a teacher trainer and a curriculum developer. His main research interests are ESP, academic writing and genre analysis. He has designed several ESP courses particulary to teach English in military establishments in Sri Lanka.
Ganga Herath
Ganga Herath is an academic attached to Department of Languages of Faculty of Management, Social Sciences and Humanities of General Sir John Kotelawala Defence University, Sri Lanka. She is the course coordinator of BA in TESOL degree programme. Previously, she worked for the Ministry of Education as a Master Trainer (English) and an e-moderator (English for Teaching programme)
Esala Erangi Malawithanthri
Esala Erangi Malawithanthri is an academic attached to the Department of Languages of Faculty of Management, Social Sciences and Humanities of General Sir John Kotelawala Defence University, Sri Lanka. She possesses MA in Linguistics, BA in English, PGDE in TESL, National Diploma in TESL, TKT CLIL etc. She has also earned both local and international qualifications related to teacher training and has been actively engaged in training English language teachers as a Master Teacher Trainer and an In-Service Advisor for numerous years. Her passion in teacher education tempts her to engage in research in English language teaching.
